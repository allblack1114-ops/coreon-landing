Opening becomes an authorized action

Most packages rely on disposable seals or trust in procedure.
COREON changes the structure so that opening requires a specific release action.

Why conventional packaging fails

Anyone can open it once accessible
Tamper seals destroy usability
External locks complicate logistics
Responsibility cannot be traced
Children & unauthorized users access
Repackaging becomes possible

COREON Structural Mechanism

Instead of adding a security device, the opening path itself is restricted. The key access area is hidden and requires a specific release motion.

Hidden key-access groove
Physical prevention of random opening
Tamper evidence immediately visible
Reusable without destruction
